Four reasons this is not an ordinary retail franchise.

Most franchises sell you a playbook. This one hands you a fifty-year brand, its factory, and its demand engine, then protects the ground you build on.

The head start

Fifty years of brand equity

Larry Stick opened the first store on Telephone Road in Houston in 1975. Half a century later, Billiard Factory is one of the nation's largest billiard and game-room furniture retailers, and the story still belongs to one family. You open with a reputation most retailers spend decades earning.

In the frame: the sign that has meant something in Texas since 1975.

The moat

Factory-direct margins

Most retailers only sell. We manufacture, distribute, and retail, so there is no distributor markup between the workshop and your showroom floor. We own the house. You inherit the margin.

In the frame: tables we built ourselves, waiting on the floor behind the counter.

The territory

Protected, and yours alone

Every franchise is awarded with a contractually protected, radius-based territory. No overlapping stores, and a shared-revenue model for billiardfactory.com orders inside your radius, so the national site works for you, not against you. Final radius and revenue-share terms publish with the FDD. Est · terms pending FDD

In the frame: one showroom, with no second store inside its radius.

The engine

Demand, handled nationally

The parent brand runs national campaigns, PR, and content. You deploy pre-built, pre-approved local creative from a dashboard, and qualified leads arrive with source attribution. You spend your time closing, not making ads.

In the frame: a family that walked in already knowing the name.

We make it. We curate it. We sell it.

Billiard Factory is a vertically integrated house of game-room brands. Five owned brands protect the margin; the best partner manufacturers in the category fill the showroom. As a franchise partner, you sell all of it under one roof and one standard.

What candidates ask first.

What does it cost to open a Billiard Factory showroom?+
Ownership's current estimate is $485,000 to $785,000 all-in: $200,000 to $400,000 for the showroom build-out, $250,000 to $350,000 in opening inventory, and a $35,000 franchise fee. Ongoing, plan on 2% royalty and 2% marketing fund. Candidates should show $150,000 in liquid capital. Final figures publish in the FDD.
Why is a game-room showroom a good retail business?+
Big-ticket, considered purchases with delivery and installation attached resist e-commerce commoditization: families want to see the table, feel the slate, and talk to someone who knows the category. Nine categories under one roof mean multiple tickets per household, and the products themselves last decades, which builds service and referral business.
What makes Billiard Factory different from other furniture franchises?+
Vertical integration. We own manufacturing, distribution, and retail, so franchise partners buy owned-brand product factory-direct with no distributor markup, and carry partner brands through our distribution power. Very few retail franchises own their supply chain end to end.
Do I get a protected territory?+
Yes. Every agreement includes a contractually protected, radius-based territory. Orders placed on billiardfactory.com from inside your radius are planned as shared revenue between you and the house, final terms in the FDD.
How long does it take to open?+
The path runs from qualification through FDD review, discovery day in Houston, site selection, build-out, and training. Target timelines for each stage are being finalized with the development team. TBD · timeline
What experience do I need?+
Retail, showroom, or multi-location operating experience matters more than billiards knowledge. We can teach the category; we cannot teach the discipline of running a great store. Owner-operators and experienced multi-unit groups are both considered.
What support do I get after opening?+
Ongoing field coaching, the national marketing engine with pre-built local campaigns, central merchandising and buying, distribution-center replenishment, and the shared technology stack. When the brand evolves, your local assets update centrally, so you never carry creative overhead.
How large is a showroom?+
Target footprint is 5,000 to 8,000 square feet, modeled on the current 11-showroom fleet, with roughly $250,000 of floor stock on display at opening. Site criteria publish with the FDD.
Can I own more than one territory?+
Yes. Multi-unit operators are a core candidate profile, and development agreements for multiple territories are part of the model under review. Multi-unit incentives, if offered, publish with the FDD. TBD · incentives
Do franchisees get the designer and trade program?+
The trade program (trade pricing, spec support, white-glove delivery and installation standards) is part of the concept, and designer relationships in your market are yours to build with the house behind you. Lead routing by territory is part of the demand engine.
I already own a billiards or game-room store. Is there a path for me?+
Yes, and it may be the fastest one. The brand entered Dallas–Fort Worth by converting seven independent stores in 2007, and ownership considers existing operators among the best candidates for the first territories. Tell us about your business in the form below.
Which markets are available?+
The first territories under review are Salt Lake City, El Paso, Greenville, NC, Knoxville, and Nashville, with a target of 90 franchised showrooms over the next five years. If your metro is not on that list, tell us anyway; the map is being drawn now, and early candidates help draw it.
Is this page an offer to sell me a franchise?+
No. This page is informational. A franchise is offered only through a Franchise Disclosure Document delivered in compliance with the FTC Franchise Rule and applicable state law.
